Famous Libraries in INDIA

Rampur Raza Library

Rampur Raza Library

The Rampur Raza Library is a renowned repository of Indo-Islamic cultural heritage, located in Rampur, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was originally established in the late 18th century by Nawab Faizullah Khan, the founder of the Rampur State, and later developed into a major library by his successors.

Rich Collection:

The library holds over 17,000 rare manuscripts, 205 hand-written palm-leaf manuscripts, thousands of printed books, and miniature paintings in Arabic, Persian, Urdu, Sanskrit, Hindi, and Turkish.

Manuscripts:

Some of the rarest Islamic, Persian, and Indian texts are preserved here, including copies of the Qur’an written in Kufic script, and illuminated manuscripts.

Architectural Style:

The building itself is an example of Indo-European architecture, combining Mughal and colonial influences.

Research Hub:

Scholars from around the world visit the library for its priceless resources on Islamic studies, Persian literature, science, history, and theology.

National Library of India

National Library of India

The National Library of India, located in Kolkata, West Bengal, is the largest library in India by volume and one of the most prestigious in Asia. It serves as the country’s apex library and functions as a repository of all printed materials published in India.

Establishment:

The origins trace back to 1836 with the Calcutta Public Library, but it was officially designated as the National Library of India on 1 February 1953.

Location:

It is housed in the Belvedere Estate, a historic colonial mansion in Alipore, Kolkata.

Collection:

Over 2.2 million books More than 86,000 maps Rare manuscripts in Sanskrit, Persian, Arabic, and other Indian languages Books in all major Indian and international languages

Legal Deposit Library:

Under the Delivery of Books and Newspapers (Public Libraries) Act, 1954, it receives a copy of every book, newspaper, and magazine published in India.

Services:

The library is a major center for research, especially in humanities and social sciences, and is open to the public for study and reference.

Anna Centenary Library

Anna Centenary Library

The Anna Centenary Library (ACL), located in Kotturpuram, Chennai, is one of India's most prominent public libraries. Inaugurated on September 15, 2010, to commemorate the 102nd birth anniversary of former Tamil Nadu Chief Minister C. N. Annadurai, the library stands as a testament to the state's commitment to education and culture

Braille Section:

Equipped with 1,500 printed Braille books, 145 e-books, and 1,080 audiobooks, facilitating access for visually impaired readers.

Children's Section:

Spanning 15,000 sq. ft., this area offers over 60,000 books in various languages, along with multimedia resources and interactive programs .

Periodicals Section:

Provides access to 37 leading newspapers and over 500 Indian and foreign magazines across various categories.

Language Sections:

Dedicated areas for Tamil and English books, encompassing a vast collection across multiple disciplines.
